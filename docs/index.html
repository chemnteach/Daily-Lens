Five European Nations Blame Russia for Navalny's Death
Five European nations—the United Kingdom, France, Germany, Sweden, and the Netherlands—have jointly accused Russia of poisoning opposition leader Alexei Navalny with a deadly toxin derived from Ecuadorian poison dart frogs. Navalny died in February 2024 at age 47 while imprisoned in Russia, serving a 19-year sentence following his conviction. The European governments' statement represents a coordinated diplomatic response assigning blame to the Kremlin for his death. Russia has not yet publicly responded to these allegations. The claim adds to previous international accusations regarding Navalny's treatment and the circumstances surrounding his death in custody.
