DHS Suspends TSA PreCheck, Global Entry During Shutdown
The Department of Homeland Security has suspended TSA PreCheck and Global Entry programs as a partial government shutdown extends into its second week. These expedited airport security programs, which allow registered travelers to pass through security screening faster, will pause starting Sunday due to staffing and operational constraints. While some Democrats characterize the suspension as counterproductive, the DHS cites the shutdown's impact on agency operations. The suspension affects millions of enrolled travelers who pay annual fees for the convenience of faster screening at U.S. airports.

Hochul Suspends NY Liquor Laws for Olympic Hockey Gold Game
New York Governor Kathy Hochul has directed the State Liquor Authority to suspend enforcement of early opening restrictions, allowing bars and restaurants to open early for the U.S. men's hockey team's gold medal game against Canada during the Olympics. The relaxation applies to most of the state, though counties under a state of emergency due to an incoming blizzard are excluded from the exemption. This temporary measure enables establishments to serve customers during the morning competition.

The Supreme Court ruled Friday that President Trump exceeded his authority under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act in implementing tariffs. The rapid move demonstrates the ongoing tension between executive power and judicial oversight regarding trade policy, with the administration seeking to maintain tariff increases through alternative constitutional channels following the court's decision.
